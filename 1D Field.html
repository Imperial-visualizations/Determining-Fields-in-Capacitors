A capacitor constructed from two parallel plates will have a constant electric field between the two plates. The Electric field magnitude in the vacuum is given by \( E = V/d\), where the separation between plates \(d\), is much smaller than the plate size as to avoid edge effects. When a Dielectric material is introduced between the two plates the fields change. Inside the dielectric, a Polarisation field will be created given by, $$\mathbf{P} = \chi_{e}\varepsilon_{0}\mathbf{E_{Dielectric}}$$ where \(\chi_{e}\) is the electric susptability of the dielectric. The relative permittivity of the dielectric is given by \( \varepsilon_{r}= 1 + \chi_{e} \). The D Field, "electric displacement", is defined as $$\mathbf{D} = \varepsilon_{0}\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{P} = \varepsilon_{r}\mathbf{E}$$


Note: The units of the E-field are different to those of the P and D fields, hence field line density only provides a qualitative representation of field strength
